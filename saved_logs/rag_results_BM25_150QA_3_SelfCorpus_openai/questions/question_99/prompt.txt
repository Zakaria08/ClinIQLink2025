Vulvar mucosal changes, vaginal discharge, and occasionally vaginal bleeding may accompany these changes. Ultrasonography represents the initial imaging modality, with larger tumors occasionally visible on abdominal radiographs as calcified masses. Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors, by contrast, typically produce androgens rather than estrogens, causing virilization rather than feminization, and occur more commonly in adolescents and young adults rather than young children.
